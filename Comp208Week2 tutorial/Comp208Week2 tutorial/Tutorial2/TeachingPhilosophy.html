My Teaching Philosophy

What inspired Me to Pursure Teaching

My name is Bianca Gage and there are many reasons as to why i decided to become a teacher. Coming from a second generation import family on both sides with stories of their lives being similar, my grandparents had migrated from croatia to Australia to escape the war and persue a better life for themselves and their family, they moved with very little money and spoke broken English so it was to no surprise the only jobs they could find were in factories, they worked consistently sometimes even working 15 hour days to raise a family, they did whatever they could to ensure that there children (my parents) could have a better life than one that they had experienced, one way was engraining into my parents the importance of education to being successful and finding a quality job later in life. An opportunity that they never had. My parents understood the importance of education, passing down this notion to my siblings.

I started my educational journey in 2008 at Our lady of Fatima Caringbah and can't remember much from primary school but do remember just how much i loved school. I loved learning new things, being with other children and being creative. I always tried my best and remember art being my favourite subject throughout my primary school life. I wasnt the best at school, but wasnt the worst either i would have classed myself average in comparison with other students. I overall really enjoyed my experience in primary school all of my teachers were really nice and really inspired me to become a teacher, i loved how they could inspire a classfull of students to be the best that they could be and push people to reach their full potential. I best learned when i could be creative such as drawing painting or when i could create my own body of work, and often disliked doing things that werent creative like maths or science thus i felt most valued and at ease when i could create. I can remember one time in year 5 when my art teacher who was one of my biggest role models and inspiration to becoming a teacher told me i came runner up for the harmony day art competition and i came straight home and was so excited to tell my parents they were so proud of me, and they straight away called their parents, my grandparents the news that i came runner up for my artwork they too were so proud of me and it made me feel so special of how proud i had made them, i didnt even care about the award or not winning i just loved how happy i had made both my parents and my grandparents. I was so inspired to becoming a teacher because if it was not for my art teacher choosing myself to partake in that competition i probably would have never really believed inmyself that i was good at art and being creative and would have wasted my potential. However that is really all i can remember from primary school.

Highschool however was a completely different story, i started off at OLMC buranneer from 7-10 and then completed year 11 & 12 at de la salle cronulla. I liked school from years 7-9 because it was still really chill and we did many creative things in our classes such as art and design and technology, my two favourite subjects throughout school. My teachers were always really nice to me at this time and i was always receiving awards for academic achievement, and i was still inspired to become a teacher. However during year 10 I was simply over school because this was the time that every conversation we had at school was about preparing for the hsc I wanted to be able to be creative and think for myself rather than teachers telling me how to think, that you had to do an essay like this to get a good mark, you have to do these subjects to get a good atar. So at this point i lost a lot of motivation in trying because i was just over the school system i never received any awards and my grades dropped from a’s and b’s to predominantly c’s with a few b’s, i rarely did my homework and often got detentions for uncompleted homework or talking in class, and i was on the verge of dropping out or completing a non atar course simply to get out of school.

However the transition to year 11 and twelve at a new school was great because it was a fresh start and i was able to pick subjects that i was interested in. One of these being design and technology. This was the best decision i had ever made as this class was the one class that really encouraged me to get back into education and how much i loved learning. My teacher miss gamsaragan was the best teacher anyone could ever ask for she was the reason as to why i wanted to pursue a career in design and teachnology teaching, her classes wernt normal classes where the teacher teaches a concept and gave out worksheets etc, she was passionate and you could tell she loved what she did, she catered to every students needs and had a personal connection to each student, she encouraged everyone to do their best and truly believed that everyone in the class could be successful and never made anyone feel dumb or that they werent good enough. Something that i want to carry on to when i become a teacher i can honestly say that without her i wouldnt have went as good as i did in her class as what i did if i didnt have her as a teacher because she was truly an inspiring person to watch teach as she just put so much effort into what she did and went above and beyond for each person in the class.
